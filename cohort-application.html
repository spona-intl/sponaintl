🔄 1. The Self-Paced Program Structure

We understand that secondary school students face demanding academic workloads. This program features a flexible, self-paced architecture with no mandatory weekly live video classes. You manage your own hours, balancing your cohort tasks with your local school schedule.

🎬 Orientation Week (Kick-Off)

To align our global team, the cohort begins with a brief, 15-minute live orientation meeting on Discord. Multiple time slots are offered to accommodate all global time zones. All sessions are recorded so students can watch the archive if they cannot attend live due to scheduling conflicts. Following this initial week, the remainder of the 6-month program transitions to a 100% self-paced workflow.

⏱️ Month-over-Month Milestones & Team Rotations

The cohort consists of 20 selected international students, split into 4 specialized tracks of 5 students each. To maximize your international networking, teams rotate regularly. You will change groups throughout the 6-month cycle to collaborate with fresh teammates across the cohort.

Every single month, your current 5-person group will collaborate independently to produce and publish a cohesive neuroscience multimedia project. The elements your team is responsible for managing include:

  • Scientific Literature Review: A research paper breaking down a specific brain science topic.
  • Educational Animation: A short, high-quality video explaining that month's research.
  • Target Language Translation: Translation of the core paper into one secondary language of your choice to drive regional access.
  • Media Kit & Content: Accompanying graphic design assets and written educational social posts.

📦 2. Submission Rules & Graduation Requirements

While the program runs on a monthly project cycle, we offer individual flexibility to accommodate your personal school exams, assignments, and holidays:

  • Submission Flexibility: Students have the freedom to submit more or fewer individual project assets depending on their monthly availability.
  • Graduation Requirement: To successfully graduate from the program and receive your official certificate, you must actively contribute to at least 6 complete projects across the 6-month cycle.
  • The Review Rule: Before any content goes live, it must enter a mandatory, text-based peer review window where all 5 team members log in independently to cross-check facts and sign off on edits.

📈 3. Verifiable Portfolio Gains & University Admissions Protocol

By maintaining consistency across this 6-month cycle and collaborating with a diverse group of global peers, you will exit the program with highly disciplined project management skills, a vast international network, and a robust, verified portfolio of published research—a critical advantage for competitive university applications.

⚡ Exceptional Case Protocol (Early Graduation)

We recognize that college application timelines vary globally. In exceptional cases where a student needs to include their verified SPONA credentials on a CV for imminent university admissions, early graduation is permitted.

  • Requirements: The student can still complete the work at their own pace, but must contribute to a minimum of 3 complete projects.
  • Mandatory Action: The student must notify their supervisor prior to their university admissions deadline so the supervisor can contact Headquarters (HQ) for formal registry verification and expedite the early graduation documentation.
